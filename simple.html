Manage a Backlog

Prioritize, refine, and create shared understanding of upcoming work.

  • Yes, And Rule

    Build on each idea without negation; add constraints after expansion.

    Why it helps: Encourages psychological safety and expands option space before prioritizing.

  • Three Pillars Pitch Game

    Each person pitches a backlog item with three supporting reasons.

    Why it helps: Forces clarity and comparable framing for prioritization discussions.

Manage Stakeholders

Align expectations, reveal concerns, and co-create next steps.

  • Status Switch Exercise

    Roleplay the conversation switching high/low status to surface dynamics.

    Why it helps: Builds empathy and strategies for navigating power and influence.

  • Last Word Game

    One person summarizes the other's point before adding their own.

    Why it helps: Practices active listening and reduces defensive responses.

Create a Product Roadmap

Generate, shape, and sequence themes into a coherent story.

  • Story Spine Structure

    Use Once upon a time → Every day → Until one day → ... → Ever since then.

    Why it helps: Turns a set of features into a narrative arc stakeholders can follow.

  • Hot Spotting Warm-up

    Quickly mark items that evoke energy or concern; cluster and label.

    Why it helps: Surfaces signal for sequencing and risk planning.

Run Product Discovery

Explore opportunity space and test assumptions with users.

  • Characters & Wants Exercise

    Define user-as-character, their want, obstacle, and tactics.

    Why it helps: Centers user motivation and reveals unmet needs.

  • What Else? Rule

    After each idea, ask "what else?" for one more beat.

    Why it helps: Pushes beyond first-order ideas to novel options.

Facilitate a Retro

Reflect on process, celebrate wins, and agree on improvements.

  • Gift-Giving Game

    Pair up and give each other imaginary gifts; explain why it fits.

    Why it helps: Builds positivity and appreciation before harder conversations.

  • One-Minute Scenes Exercise

    Act out the sprint as a scene; identify beats that went well/poorly.

    Why it helps: Externalizes events for objective discussion.

Define Product Strategy

Clarify positioning, bets, and principles.

  • Taglines Blitz Game

    Generate rapid taglines for the product in 3 styles; vote on themes.

    Why it helps: Reveals distinct positions and resonates with stakeholders quickly.

  • Obvious-Nonobvious Exercise

    List obvious moves, then force non-obvious opposites.

    Why it helps: Avoids me-too strategies and highlights contrarian opportunities.

Run a Workshop

Design a high-energy session that produces decisions and artifacts.

  • Conducted Story Game

    Facilitator points to people to continue one story; enforce brevity.

    Why it helps: Keeps energy high and voices balanced while converging on narrative.

  • Constraints Ladder Structure

    Iteratively add constraints to improve and focus ideas.

    Why it helps: Moves group from divergence to convergence cleanly.

About
